Calendar Week 18 -
here's what happened

Job report surprises, commodities dragging and a potential trade solution in sight? April's volatility gave way to cautious optimism - but the macro risks haven't disappeared.

April 2025 has been a month of high volatility — with some investors now already reaping "greenish" rewards if they diamond-handed through it, while others are still catching their breath from the rollercoaster of the past few months. But it has also been a month of shifting winds compared to the macroeconomic weather the now more than 100-day-old U.S. administration has bestowed upon us since taking office in January.

Jobs & PMI Data

This week's jobs data for April came in quite strong: the U.S. jobs report showed 177,000 added non-farm payroll jobs, surpassing expectations and buoying the rally in equities. The U.S. manufacturing PMI came in at 50.2 — unchanged from the previous month — signalling just marginal expansion. The services PMI, however, actually suggests slowing growth, having dropped from 54.4 in March to 51.4.

So although we're seeing an apparent rebound in equities and many other assets from March continuing into May, with optimism rising again, the overall fear of stagflation and the present geopolitical tension throughout April has not been fully erased — and still lingers around the edges of markets.

Safe Havens & Crypto

This prevailing uncertainty has been mirrored in the continued rallies of "safe-haven" assets — Gold and Silver — but also, notably, Bitcoin, up 30% from its April lows. With Bitcoin typically moving more or less inverse to gold, it has proven itself a surprisingly welcoming haven to investors in these times.

This can be pinned partly to increased institutional interest: MicroStrategy offering up to $21B in equity to acquire more BTC, and Morgan Stanley's potential entry into spot crypto trading. These effects have trickled down to smaller-cap cryptos as well — Solana (+54%) and Sui (+112%) both showcasing once again the dynamic nature of crypto markets and the potential for rapid shifts in investor sentiment.

Commodities

Opposite to the uptrend in crypto, commodities took a hit. Brent Oil demand lingered as news of easing trade tensions between the U.S. and China created slight optimism around future demand. Corn and Grains were down due to seasonality and weak demand signals — though a reversal later in the year could be expected if historical patterns prove reliable.

Conclusion

May kicked off with a risk-on rebound in global markets, recovering from April's volatility. Job data came in strong, trade tensions are softening, and investor sentiment is rising. Commodities are lagging due to weak demand, but crypto is at the forefront of the uptrend — with various altcoins recording triple-digit performances and overall strong market beta.

Personally, I remain optimistic for May in general. A slight dip in crypto would not shock me, but the overall trend seems to be up. For equities as well, I think we can expect continuous market activity — and depending on monetary policy, geopolitical agreements and global liquidity, even more upside. I shall remain bullish.

With that — have a great week.

Lennart Rose May 4, 2025
