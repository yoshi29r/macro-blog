Blog Overview
& Approach

How this blog is structured, which indicators I track, and why. Guided by a simple principle: if you can't explain something simply, you don't understand it well enough.

As a framework for my writing, I focus on five core topics — to stay consistent, clear, and above all, simple. Always guided by the principle attributed to Einstein: "If you can't explain something simply, you don't understand it well enough."

The Five Blocks

Every post is structured around the same five areas:

1. Liquidity

What I track: Fed Balance Sheet, Reverse Repo (RRP), Treasury General Account (TGA), Global M2.
Formula: Net Liquidity = Fed Balance Sheet – TGA – RRP
This tells us how much "free" money is circulating in the financial system. When net liquidity rises, risk assets tend to follow. When it falls, everything tightens.

2. Rates

What I track: US 2Y Yield, US 10Y Yield, Yield Curve (10Y – 2Y).
The shape of the yield curve is one of the most reliable signals in macro. When longer-maturity bonds yield significantly more than shorter ones, it reflects investor anxiety about the future — often for geopolitical or inflationary reasons. When the curve inverts, recession risk rises.

3. Dollar

What I track: DXY, its relationship to Emerging Markets, commodities, Bitcoin and global liquidity.
Dollar strength is not just a FX story — it affects the cost of servicing dollar-denominated debt worldwide, and tightens financial conditions globally when it rises. Understanding where the dollar is headed is central to understanding global macro.

4. Commodities

What I track: Gold and Oil (Brent).
Gold signals fear, uncertainty, and distrust of fiat currency. Oil signals global growth expectations and geopolitical tension. Together, they often tell the story before equities catch on.

5. Risk Assets

What I track: S&P 500, Nasdaq, Bitcoin, selected Altcoins.
The final read on where sentiment stands. Risk assets are the thermometer of market confidence — when they diverge from macro fundamentals, something is either mispriced or about to move.

Post Structure

Every update follows the same format:

Macro Update — [Date]
Liquidity → Rates → Dollar → Commodities → Markets → Conclusion

Where relevant, I'll add an Open Question of the Day — a specific mechanism or relationship I'm thinking through and haven't fully resolved yet.

Data Sources

TradingView, FRED (RRP, TGA, Fed Balance Sheet, US 2Y, US 10Y, DXY), Barchart, US Debt Clock, Liquidity Indices, Twitter/X.

This blog is not investment advice. It is a thinking tool — a way to make sense of complex macro dynamics by writing them down and questioning them.
